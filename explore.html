Compassion


In a showery evening,
Across the road I saw the last bus for the day
With the hope to still catch the bus
Running through the drizzly lanes
I hastily jumped over the potholes

Finally I reached the bus
And I’m thankful for a reason,
Not just coz I got the bus
But because I also got a space
A vacant seat? Not possible
I got a space to stand!

Just before me, was a man in a seat
Roughly around 50 maybe?
Impatiently wanting to get up.
Thought he was getting down at the next stop
But he didn’t
He looked behind, gazed, stood up a bit
And sat down again

I wonder what he was up to
He finally got up in the next stop
Turned around and asked me to take his seat
I agreed
Little did I know, it wasn’t his stop at all
“I cannot just sit down when I know a girl is standing there”
He said.
Taking a seat of an old man didn’t feel right
I resisted but he insisted me to sit down
“You really look tired; I can stand for a while”
I joined my hands and bowed a bit
Expressed my gratitude.
